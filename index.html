02 Virus Pathophysiology

It is vital to distinguish between HIV and AIDS. HIV is the Human Immunodeficiency Virus that compromises cellular immunity by targeting host lymphocytes. AIDS is the clinical syndrome of severe immunosuppression. Note that the Window Period, where detection is impossible, lasts approximately ninety days post-exposure.

03 The ESSE Transmission Logic

HIV transmission is governed by the E S S E principle. First, the virus must Exit the host. Second, it must Survive environmental exposure. Third, it must be present in Sufficient quantities. Finally, it must have an Entry point into the new host system.

04 U equals U Protocol

The gold standard of modern HIV care is the U equals U principle: Undetectable equals Untransmittable. When a patient achieves a suppressed viral load through consistent antiretroviral therapy, the risk of sexual transmission is effectively zero. This is the cornerstone of ending the epidemic.
